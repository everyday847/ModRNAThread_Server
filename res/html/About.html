ModRNAThread Server

Individual RNA backbone scaffolds exhibit considerable consistency across multiple species and therefore RNA sequences. Furthermore, structures commonly exist for only a fraction of the sequences known for a particular molecule, such as the tRNA employed for a given amino acid. Furthermore, natural RNA molecules, particularly structurall critical ones, frequently contain chemical modifications that have historically been neglected in structural modeling, with few exceptions, either because the experimental data could not sufficiently support their presence or because their structural ramifications were poorly understood. To this end, the ModRNAThread server is intended to permit users to thread a new sequence--longer or shorter, more or less heavily chemically modified--onto a backbone scaffold of their choice.
